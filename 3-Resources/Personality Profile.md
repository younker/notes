# Personality and Cognitive Profile

## 1. Attention and Focus
- **State-dependent attention:** Difficulty sustaining focus on low-interest tasks (e.g., reading, verbal instructions).  
- **Hyperfocus:** Strong immersion on intrinsically motivating tasks (e.g., puzzles, selected projects).  
- **Auditory/working memory limitations:** Struggle with sequences of more than 2–3 verbal instructions.  
- **Sensory grounding:** Calm and focused in slow, low-stimulation environments (nature, leaves moving, slow-paced media).  

## 2. Cognitive Performance
- **Freezing under cognitive stress:** Difficulty performing in high-evaluation, multi-step cognitive situations.  
- **Excels under physical stress:** Performs well in physically demanding tasks or embodied challenges.  
- **High intellectual capacity:** Enjoys complex problem-solving and abstract thinking (INTP tendencies).  

## 3. Emotional and Social Traits
- **Emotional sensitivity:** Overly reactive to feedback; ruminates despite logical understanding that criticism is not personal.  
- **Conflict sensitivity:** Low tolerance for trivial interpersonal conflicts (e.g., minor family disagreements).  
- **Mood variability:** Brief periods of elevated drive (hypomania) followed by low mood (dysthymia) lasting a few days.  
- **Need for solitude:** Requires significant alone-time for emotional and cognitive regulation.  

## 4. Personality and Temperament
- **Introverted and reflective:** Prefers autonomy and minimal social noise.  
- **INTP tendencies:** Analytical, abstract thinker, enjoys puzzles and conceptual exploration.  
- **High sensitivity to coherence and meaning:** Irritated by incoherence, triviality, or inefficiency.  
- **Stimulation mismatch:** Caffeine has little effect; attention and energy are regulated by intrinsic interest rather than external arousal.  

## 5. Behavioral Patterns
- **Reading and learning:** Tends to “read without hearing”; attention drifts when content is uninteresting.  
- **Task performance:** Excels when tasks are structured, meaningful, and self-directed.  
- **Emotional regulation:** Works hard to maintain composure in competitive or evaluative contexts.  

## 6. Cognitive-Emotional Unifying Frame
> Your mind is optimized for **depth, pattern recognition, and autonomy**, but becomes dysregulated by **imposed sequencing, verbal load, and low-meaning social noise**.  
> When environmental conditions align with intrinsic motivation and structure, you perform exceptionally well. When they do not, attention, emotional stability, and cognitive performance can be impaired.  

## 7. ADHD-Relevant Features
- Difficulty sustaining attention on low-interest tasks  
- Hyperfocus on high-interest tasks  
- Working memory/sequencing challenges  
- Emotional sensitivity and rumination  
- Strong need for solitude and self-directed structure  

> **Note:** Your profile partially overlaps with ADHD patterns, but mood variability and context-dependent regulation suggest additional temperament or neurodivergent factors.
