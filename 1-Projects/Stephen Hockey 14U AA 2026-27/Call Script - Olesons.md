# Call Script — Oleson's Food Store

For asking Oleson's to sponsor the [[Golf Outing Fundraiser|14UAA Golf Outing]]. Listed in the Mid-Sized Donor tier on [[Golf Outing Donor List]] — target ask is **Hat-Trick ($500)** with **General ($250)** as a soft fallback. Bigger asks are fine if the conversation opens up.

## Before You Dial

- Sit somewhere quiet, have a pen, have the [[Golf Outing Fundraiser]] note open for facts.
- Goal of the first call: get the **store manager's name + best path to a yes** (direct email or follow-up time). A pledge on the first call is a bonus, not the goal.
- Time-sensitive hook: **signage deadline is August 3** — that's the reason you're calling now and not later.

## Reception Opener

> "Hi, this is Jason Younker. I'm a parent with the Traverse City North Stars 14U AA hockey team. We're putting on our annual golf fundraiser in August and I'm reaching out to local businesses about sponsorship. Could you point me to the store manager — or whoever handles community sponsorship requests?"

If reception offers to take a message:

> "Sure — if you could let them know it's about a local youth hockey sponsorship and our signage deadline is August 3rd, I'd appreciate it. Best number to reach me is 206-795-4418. I'm also happy to email the details directly if there's a good address."

## Manager Opener

> "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the call. I'm Jason Younker, parent on the 14U AA North Stars travel hockey team here in Traverse City. We hold an annual golf fundraiser every August at Grand Traverse Resort — this year it's August 16th — and it covers a big chunk of our season costs: ice time, tournaments, travel. We're reaching out to local businesses Oleson's included because you've been a part of this community for a long time and a lot of our hockey families shop with you. Got a minute for me to walk through what sponsorship looks like?"

## The Pitch (60 seconds)

> "We've got four sponsorship levels. The one that fits most local businesses is **Hat-Trick at $500** — that gets your name on signage at an event hole the day of the outing, in front of every team in the scramble. There's also a **General level at $250** that puts you on a hole or tee sign. The bigger packages — Playmaker at $1,000 and Stanley Cup at $2,000 — include more signage and the Stanley Cup includes golf and lunch for a foursome.
>
> Grand Traverse Hockey Association is a 501(c)(3), so it's a fully deductible donation. EIN's on the form. I can email or drop off the sponsorship sheet today — what works better for you?"

## Close

Pick the strongest available:

1. **Yes today** → "Awesome. I'll send the form to your email — just need business name, contact, address, and phone. Checks made out to GTHA, or Venmo works too. Could you have it back to me before August 3rd so we can get your name on the signage?"
2. **Need to check / think about it** → "Totally understand. What's the best way to follow up — call back next week, or is there an email I can send the sheet to so you've got it in front of you? When's a good day to circle back?"
3. **Not this year** → "No worries, appreciate you taking the call. Mind if I check back next year? And if you know of any other local businesses we should be talking to, I'd take a tip."

## Likely Objections

| They say | You say |
|---|---|
| "We get a lot of these requests." | "I get it — we're trying to be respectful of that, which is why we keep the smallest level at $250 with real visibility. Even at General your sign is in front of every foursome on a hole all morning." |
| "We need it in writing." | "Of course — I'll email the sponsorship form right now. What's the best address?" |
| "Can you stop by?" | "Yeah, I can drop the form off. What day this week works?" |
| "What does the money go to?" | "Travel hockey is expensive — ice time, league fees, tournament entries, sometimes hotel costs for out-of-town tournaments. The golf outing is the team's biggest fundraiser, so every sponsorship directly offsets what families would otherwise pay out of pocket." |
| "Can we donate product / gift cards instead of cash?" | "Let me check with our organizer Matthew Wheeler and get back to you — I'd guess yes, especially for the prize tables, but I want to confirm before I commit." |
