# Call Script — Traverse City Chamber

For [[Golf Outing Fundraiser|14UAA Golf Outing]]. The TC area chamber **may now be branded as "Traverse Connect"** (chamber + regional economic-development functions merged some years back). Confirm the org name before dialing. Find current main number at traverseconnect.com or the chamber's listed contact page.

This is a **two-part ask**, not a single sponsorship ask:

1. **Modest direct donation** — $250 General level, or even a smaller goodwill amount
2. **Referrals** — names of 5 member businesses with formal community-giving / corporate-sponsorship programs

The referrals are the real prize. The donation is the down payment that earns the right to ask.

## Before You Dial

- Have the [[Golf Outing Fundraiser]] details open.
- You're not trying to sell. You're asking the chamber to do what chambers exist to do: **connect a local effort with member businesses.** That framing makes them feel useful, not solicited.
- Right contact title is unclear — likely **Community Engagement**, **Membership Director**, **Public Affairs**, or **President/CEO**. Let reception route you.

## Reception Opener

> "Hi, this is Jason Younker. I'm a hockey parent with the TC North Stars 14U AA travel team — we're putting on our annual golf fundraiser in August and I'm trying to connect with someone at the chamber about two things: a small sponsorship and some guidance on which member businesses to talk to. Who would be the right person — community engagement, membership, or someone else?"

If reception offers to take a message:

> "Sure — let them know it's about local youth sports, looking for both a chamber-level sponsorship and a few member referrals. Our signage deadline is August 3rd, so the sooner I can connect the better. Best callback is 206-795-4418."

## Right-Person Opener

> "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the call. I'm Jason Younker — hockey parent with the TC North Stars 14U AA team. We do an annual golf scramble fundraiser every August at Grand Traverse Resort, and the chamber is on my call list for two reasons. First, I'd like to invite the chamber to sponsor at whatever level makes sense — and second, more importantly, I'd love your help identifying member businesses that already run formal community-giving programs. Got 5 minutes?"

## The Ask (60 seconds)

> "The fundraiser is **August 16, scramble format, Spruce Run at Grand Traverse Resort, 9 AM shotgun**. Sponsorship tiers run **$250 General up to $2,000 Stanley Cup**, and signage deadline is **August 3rd**. GTHA — Grand Traverse Hockey Association — is the 501(c)(3) sponsor of record, EIN 38-3347536.
>
> On the sponsorship side, I'd love to put the chamber's name on a hole or tee sign at whatever level fits — even a goodwill General at $250 would be meaningful to the team.
>
> On the **referral side** — this is the bigger ask — you know your members better than anyone. I've got a list of obvious targets like the auto dealers and the credit unions, but the businesses I'm probably missing are the ones with **formal corporate-giving programs** for youth sports or community events. If you could point me to 5 members who run those programs, you'd save me a week of cold calling and we'd raise more money. Is that something you'd be willing to help with?"

## Close

Pick the strongest available:

1. **Yes to both** → "That's fantastic. For the donation, I'll email the sponsorship sheet today — easiest thing. For the referrals, what works best: email me a few names, or set up a quick coffee where you can talk me through which members to approach and how?"
2. **Yes to referrals only** → "Totally understand — and honestly, the referrals are worth more to us than a donation. What's the best way to follow up — email a list, or pull me into your next member newsletter as a one-line mention?"
3. **Donation yes, referrals "I'll think about it"** → "No problem. While we're talking — even just two or three member names would help me prioritize. Anyone come to mind off the top of your head?"
4. **No to both, polite** → "Got it, appreciate the time. Is there a community-events newsletter or board where local fundraisers like ours can post? Anything that puts us in front of members."

## Likely Objections

| They say | You say |
|---|---|
| "We don't sponsor directly — we connect, we don't fund." | "That works for me — honestly the referrals are the more valuable ask anyway. Who comes to mind among your members with active community-giving programs?" |
| "Can you put it in writing?" | "Of course — what's the best email? I'll send the sponsorship sheet and a one-page summary you can forward if anyone on your team wants to see it." |
| "We can mention you in the member newsletter." | "That would be huge — when does the next one go out? Happy to give you a one-paragraph blurb to drop in." |
| "Have you tried [obvious business]?" | "Yep, they're on my list. What I'm hoping for from you is the *non-obvious* members — businesses that run formal giving programs that I wouldn't know about from the outside." |
| "Submit a request through our website." | "Will do. Anyone on your team I should follow up with by name after I submit, so it doesn't sit in the queue?" |
