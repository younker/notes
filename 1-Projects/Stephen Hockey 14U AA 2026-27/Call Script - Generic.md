For asking any local business to sponsor the [[Golf Outing Fundraiser|14UAA Golf Outing]]. Fill in the business specifics before dialing. Update [[Golf Outing Donor List]] after the call.

**Before each call, fill in:**

- **Business**: ________________

- **Target contact title**: ________________ (GM, Owner, Branch Manager, Community Relations Director, etc.)
- **Tier target**: ____ Stanley Cup $2,000 · ____ Playmaker $1,000 · ____ Hat-Trick $500 · ____ General $250
- **Fallback tier**: ________________

Match the tier to the business size — auto dealers, hospitals, and big regionals get the Stanley Cup or Playmaker pitch; mid-sized local businesses get Hat-Trick with General as fallback; small / first-ask businesses start at General.

## Before You Dial

- Quiet room, pen ready, [[Golf Outing Fundraiser]] open.
- **First call goal**: get the right contact's name + a clear path to a yes (direct email or follow-up time). A pledge is a bonus, not the goal.
- **Time hook**: signage deadline is **August 3** — that's why you're calling now and not later.

## Reception Opener

> "Hi, this is Jason Younker. I'm a parent with the Traverse City North Stars 14U AA hockey team. We're putting on our annual golf fundraiser in August and I'm reaching out to local businesses about sponsorship. Could you point me to [TARGET CONTACT TITLE] — or whoever handles community sponsorship requests?"

If reception offers to take a message:

> "Sure — let them know it's about a local youth hockey sponsorship and our signage deadline is August 3rd. Best callback is 206-795-4418. I'm also happy to email the details if there's a good address."

## Right-Person Opener

> "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the call. I'm Jason Younker, parent on the 14U AA North Stars travel hockey team. We hold an annual golf fundraiser every August at Grand Traverse Resort — this year it's August 16th — and it covers a big part of our season costs: ice time, tournaments, travel. We're reaching out to local businesses that have a strong community presence here. Got a minute for me to walk through what sponsorship looks like?"

## The Pitch (60 seconds)

> "We've got four sponsorship levels:
>
> - **Stanley Cup at $2,000** — includes golf and lunch for a foursome plus primary contest signage
> - **Playmaker at $1,000** — primary contest signage at the fundraiser
> - **Hat-Trick at $500** — event hole signage
> - **General at $250** — hole or tee signage
>
> For most local businesses the sweet spot is **Hat-Trick or General** — but if a higher level fits your community-giving budget, even better. Whatever level you choose, your name goes up in front of every team in the scramble.
>
> Grand Traverse Hockey Association is a 501(c)(3), so it's a fully deductible donation. EIN's on the form. I can email or drop off the sponsorship sheet today — what works better for you?"

## Close

Pick the strongest available:

1. **Yes today** → "Awesome. I'll send the form to your email — just need business name, contact, address, and phone. Checks made out to GTHA, or Venmo works too. Can you get it back to me before August 3rd so we make the signage?"
2. **Need to check / think about it** → "Totally understand. What's the best way to follow up — call back next week, or is there an email I can send the sheet to? When's a good day to circle back?"
3. **Not this year** → "No worries, appreciate you taking the call. Mind if I check back next year? And if you know of any other local businesses we should be talking to, I'd take a tip."

## Likely Objections

|---|---|
| "We get a lot of these requests." | "I get it — we're trying to be respectful of that, which is why we keep the smallest level at $250 with real visibility. Even at General your sign is in front of every foursome on a hole all morning." |
| "We need it in writing." | "Of course — I'll email the sponsorship form right now. What's the best address?" |
| "Can you stop by?" | "Yeah, I can drop the form off. What day this week works?" |
| "What does the money go to?" | "Travel hockey is expensive — ice time, league fees, tournament entries, sometimes hotel costs for out-of-town tournaments. The golf outing is the team's biggest fundraiser, so every sponsorship directly offsets what families would otherwise pay out of pocket." |
| "Can we donate product / gift cards instead of cash?" | "Let me check with our organizer Matthew Wheeler and get back to you — I'd guess yes, especially for the prize tables, but I want to confirm before I commit." |
| "Who else is sponsoring?" | "Several other local businesses have already committed at various levels. Happy to share the current list when I follow up with the form." |
