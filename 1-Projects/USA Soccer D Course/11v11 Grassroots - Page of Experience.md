# Page of Experience

## Introduction: Six Tasks of a Coach

Please describe:

1. Two things you feel good about

1a) Given my playing experience, I feel confident in reading the game and identifying player and team strengths and weaknesses.

1b) Making adjustments to better match players' skillsets, or adjustments to the formation according to how the game is playing out.

2. Two challenges you face

2a) I can over-coach from the sideline. I had a coach who always said if I have to yell at you from the sideline then I have not done my job during practice.
2b) Playing time is always hard. Balancing playing time in a league context where you balance merit with allowing everyone time can be tricky.

3. Your learning needs

To coach at a higher level, I will need to familiarize myself with more modern advancements and strategies in the game.

4. Your action steps

I'm assistant coaching to a very knowledgeable coach currently. I'm just watching and learning, trying to see what works and what does not. Also watching more soccer these days as well as YouTube (and other) instructional videos.

## Leading the Player: Unconditional, individual attention

Please describe:

1. Two things you feel good about

a) Allocating time for various drills. It's easy to place myself in their shoes and see when they need a break, water, direction, whatever.

b) Identifying things the player is good at and things they could work on, and then praising or drawing attention to it in a way that builds them up.

2. Two challenges you face

a) Working with kids I do not enjoy being with. Sorry, it's hard to enjoy coaching some kids. They push you away, are disrespectful, arrogant, mean to others, whatever.

b) I struggle when kids who do not show up to practice or simply don't try but get a large amount of playing time. I would rather lose a game and teach them than win and think that behavior is going to help them in the future.

3. Your learning needs

Given my two challenges, perhaps therapy and a little more compassion? Seriously, I am finding some of the more advanced concepts I can do but have a hard time explaining or teaching. For example I was trying to show a kid a certain move and I couldn't explain it because it was just second nature.

4. Your action steps

Constantly remind myself that even the tough kids are kids and I'm their coach, in a position of power, and need to exercise maximum restraint, patience and compassion.

## Managing the Performance Environment: Engaging Parents

Please describe:

1. Two things you feel good about

a) Managing relationships with people.
b) Getting (at least a subset of) parents to help out to lighten the load so I can focus on tactics and other non-logistical matters.

2. Two challenges you face

a) Overly competitive parents living vicariously through their kids. Parents are by far the most common part of the equation that spoils it for everyone!
b) Confrontation with parents. It's rarely easy.

3. Your learning needs

Continue working on de-escalation techniques. That is the main thing and is more than enough to keep anyone busy for a lifetime.

4. Your action steps

Reach out to parents, build a relationship.
