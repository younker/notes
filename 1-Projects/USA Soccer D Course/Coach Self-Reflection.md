## Coaching Philosophy

**Approach in one or two sentences:** Develop the player, not the result. My job is to create the conditions where kids build real competence, fall in love with the game, and want to come back next season.

**Who do you aspire to be as a coach?** Someone players remember not because we won a particular game, but because I made an impact on their love or view of the game or coaching in general.

**One value or idea that's important to you:** Long-term participation over short-term outcomes and balancing development with competition

## Motivation for Coaching

**Why did you decide to become a coach?** I love the sport and wanted to experience other aspects. The more I coached, the more it became obvious that I really enjoy it.

**Main reason kids play soccer:** Because it's fun. If it stops being fun, they stop showing up. I have seen it happen on various teams both as a player and an assistant coach. 

**What should a coach do for their players?** Help them develop both skills and a love for the game. Teach them what it means to compete with integrity and joy.

## Player-Centered Coaching

**What does 'player-centered coaching' mean?** Put the player first & identify what the players need technically, physically & emotionally

**How do you help players get better?** Age/skill appropriate practice plans, individual feedback, and a lot of touches on the ball.

**One way you get to know your players:** Spend time with them. Listen to them. Watch them play. Support them. 

**How do you let players share ideas or make choices?** I ask them what they see. I ask questions. I hand them ownership over things like warm-ups
