PHS Boys Soccer - Summer & Fall Schedule

Dear PHS Soccer Families,

Hello! My name is Jason Younker, the new varsity head coach. I have been working to get acclimated and wrap my head around various facets of the job. Given the hiring timeline, I am a bit behind the eight ball and so I ask that you extend a little patience as we get the summer schedule finalized.

I want to start out letting you all know how excited I am to step into this role. I have played, coached, and reffed soccer my entire life, so stepping back into coaching at the high school level is one I have looked forward to.

Below is the training and fall season schedule as it currently stands. While tentative, I hope it is enough to use to schedule your summer plans.

Lastly, expectations around the summer schedule are to attend what you can. I will not be setting any rosters or making team determinations during this time, but this is an opportunity for me to get to know the players and keep them sharp for the season.

Additionally, there is only 1 week between the official start of the season on Aug. 11th and our first match against Grand Blanc, a scrimmage, on Aug. 17th. These summer sessions are meant to address that short window we have to get ready. Put another way, we will not have time to get the boys in shape once the season starts; they will need to show up to the first practice ready to go.

Summer Practices

The summer is divided up into the following:

  • June 1 - June 28: Practices allowed
  • June 29 - July 5: MHSAA required dead zone, no team activity
  • July 6 - July 31: Practices allowed
  • Aug 1 - Aug 10: Conditioning week
  • Aug 11: Regular season starts

Below is a breakdown by month.

June

We have 8 practices and then 2 short conditioning sessions planned for June. The conditioning sessions will be used to assess where the players are at with their conditioning. Practices will be 90 minutes and consist of some technical work for the first 30 minutes followed by a small-sided scrimmage. They are meant to be a fun way to get touches on the ball while allowing a means to introduce myself to the boys.

June is pretty busy and the schedule reflects that. My hope is that next year I will have this out earlier and can provide a more predictable cadence.

A few notes on June:

  • The midnight scrimmage is meant to be a fun kickoff to the season and aligns with a tradition here that I would like to continue.

  • Thursday practices will be earlier in the day. This is to accommodate my work schedule but also to take advantage of the cooler part of the day. I am hopeful the boys will be onboard.
  • Ideally the boys could prioritize the 2 conditioning sessions. These will be short and provide important data to benchmark progress.
