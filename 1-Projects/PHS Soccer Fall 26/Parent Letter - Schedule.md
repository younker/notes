## Letter

Dear PHS Soccer Families,

Hello! My name is Jason Younker, the new varsity head coach. I have been working to get acclimated and wrap my head around various facets of the job. Given the hiring timeline, I am a bit behind the eight ball and so I ask that you extend a little patience as we get the summer schedule finalized.

I want to start out letting you all know how excited I am to step into this role. I have played, coached and ref'd soccer my entire life so stepping back into coaching high school is one I have looked forward to.

Below is the training and fall season schedule as it currently stands. While tentative, I would hope it is enough to use to schedule your summer plans.

Until we get a working copy in Parent Square, you can view my personal PHS calendar here:

https://tinyurl.com/phs-soccer-2026

Lastly, expectations around the summer schedule are to attend what you can. I will not be setting any rosters or making team determinations during this time, but this is an opportunity for me to get to know the players and keep them sharp for the season. Additionally, there is 1 week before the official start of the season on Aug. 11th and our first match against Grand Blanc, a scrimmage, on Aug. 17th. These summer sessions are meant to address that short window we have to get ready. Put another way, we will not have time to get the boys in shape prior to the season starting; they will need to show up to the first practice ready to go. How they acheive that is up to them, but whether they do so will effect decision made

### Summer Practices

The summer is divided up into the following:

- **June 1 -- June 28**: Practices allowed
- **June 29 -- July 5**: MHSAA dead zone, no team activity
- **July 6 -- July 31**: Practices allowed
- **Aug 1 -- Aug 10**: Conditioning week
- **Aug 11**: Regular season starts

Below is a break down by month.

Practices will be 90 minutes and consist of some technical work for the first 30 minutes followed by a small sided scrimmage. They are meant to be a fun way to get touches on the ball while allowing a means to introduce myself to the boys.

June is a pretty busy and the schedule reflects that. My hope is that next year I will have this out earlier and can provide a more predictable cadence.

### Theme

As we approach the season, it was unavoidable to notice all the change in the program. And with change, comes some instability. I want to embrace that and as I do so, I have come to give this season a theme: Doppelganger. Like a doppelganger, we will look the same to our opponents at first, but they will soon realize we are not the same team. Noah is an excellent coach and one of my goals is to provide some stability and consistency for the boys. As a result, you will see me persisting many of the traditions and practices that both Zach and Noah have introduced. Yet at the same time, I have my own style which I will introduce to the boys resulting in slightly different play and culture. This is where we begin to form a our new identity. 



### A Few Notes
