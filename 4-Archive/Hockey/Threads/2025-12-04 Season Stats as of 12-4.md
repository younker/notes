Hey Brandon, that's amazing, thank you!

So last season I used a stats template from Google Sheets to track things,
and during the many hours in the car after Silver Sticks and Thanksgiving I
downloaded it again and started plugging in our stats for this season. Some
dufus had messed up the template marked "DO NOT EDIT" but I think I got it
all fixed, although I am far from a spreadsheet power user. Brandon, maybe
you can make sense of this format to see what in it has value? I love all
the detail in yours, and a fairly quick glance at the two formats shows
they track somewhat different things so just sharing it with all for
reference.

One thing I would humbly suggest adding is a "games played" tracker or
summary sheet. That will be handy for doing the MAHA district book and is
something featured in the template I used. (I think it would just take
adding a yes/no value for each game for each player.)

I can give edit power to anyone with a google account but for now I made it
viewable to anyone with the link:
https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1JmIRNPmjtgp1TuTdoxNSO1r2y8J8Cfu2/edit?usp=sharing&ouid=116585147146239496949&rtpof=true&sd=true

Note that as the keeper of the gamesheets everything I entered has come
from there, so there will likely be differences between how the refs scored
games versus how Brandon scored them after his detailed video review. That
being said, for the couple games that I had Brandon's data I did use that
to supplement the official gamesheet when needed for stats like shots
for/against and plus/minus.

Anyway we probably don't need to keep two spreadsheets, but one thing I
started doing on mine was color coding games as league, exhibition, or
tournament, and compiling our record across those different types of games
and overall. Maybe something like that could be done via a summary sheet,
the way Goals, Assists, Points, and +/- are handled in Brandon's version?
(which I really like)

Brandon is infinitely smarter than me at this stuff, particularly getting
multiple sheets talking to each other, which is why I downloaded a template
instead of creating my own spreadsheet.

Thanks Brandon!
