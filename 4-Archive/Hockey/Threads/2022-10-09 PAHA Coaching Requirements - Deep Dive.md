> Hello! Thanks to all of you that have volunteered to coach with PAHA, our kids could not play without your time and energy. With the season just around the corner, I'm sending out an excessively detailed list of the coaching requirements to expand on what was shared earlier. Please read through it to see if you've missed anything. I'm happy to answer any questions or provide any other support you need, just reach out!
>
> Reminder: PAHA will reimburse you for your coaching certification costs, please fill out the attached form and send it to Sarah Saddison (saddisonpaving@gmail.com) along with your receipts.
>
> Step 1 - Get a 2022-23 USA Hockey number
> You are required to register with USA Hockey as a coach.
> Link: https://membership.usahockey.com/
>
> Step 2 - Send your USA Hockey number to PAHA Registrar
> Please send your USA Hockey registration confirmation and number to PAHA Registrar Liz Morey (elizabethmorey17@gmail.com) who tracks the eligibility of players and coaches.
>
> Step 3 - Create or Log Into Your USA Hockey Coaching Profile
> Link to coaching profile: https://courses.usahockey.com/public
> You MUST do this to track your various requirements and register for online courses.
> Once you are logged in, click "My Profile" and then "Coaching Information" from the menu on the upper left. This page displays all of your key information including your CEP certification, SafeSport status, and Background Screening status (more on these below).
>
> Step 4 - Complete a Background Check
> Link: https://www.usahockey.com/backgroundscreen
> From your coaching information page referenced in Step 3 you will be able to check if your screening is current; if a previous screening has expired there will be a renewal link to click.
>
> NOTE: Background checks are good for two years, so if you did a background check last year (21-22) then you don't need a new one.
>
> Step 5 - Complete SafeSport Training
> You can access this training through your coaching information page from Step 3 or this link: https://www.usahockey.com/safesporttraining
> Info is attached regarding concussions.
>
> Step 6 - Complete Age Specific Modules for the Group You Are Coaching
> The best and worst thing about USA Hockey is the level of detail in their American Development Model (ADM). On the one hand there are a lot of hoops for volunteer coaches to jump through to get certified, but on the other hand the coaching philosophy is really integrated across age levels and they provide you with a ton of materials to reference (including a full season of practice plans, dryland training ideas, etc). USA Hockey really sets up new coaches to succeed, and gives experienced coaches new ideas. One of ADM's core philosophies, which I love, is developing athletes versus developing hockey players (especially at younger ages) and focusing on love of the game. You'll hear all about it in your Age Specific Modules but the ADM is where the concept of small area drills originates along with which skills to train at what ages.
>
> REQUIREMENT - Completion of Age Specific Modules for your age group
> https://www.usahockey.com/agespecificmodules
> Basically, this is a series of short videos and quick quizzes designed to give you an overview of the key concepts, drills, etc for the age group you are coaching.
> Attached to this email is a COACHING GUIDELINES document from USA Hockey that explains a bit more.
>
> Step 7 - Review the Coaching Ethics Handbook (attached)
> There is some good stuff in there and it is pretty short so please review. As PAHA's Coaching Director, the top two things I want from coaches are pretty simple: 1) don't be a jerk (to kids or parents), 2) make hockey fun. If you do these two things you will have a good season and the kids will come back and play next year.
>
> ALL of these must be completed before you can take the ice with your team: USA Hockey number, background check, SafeSport, and your age specific modules.
> If you just want to help at practice, then that's all you need to do. If you want to be officially rostered as a coach and allowed on the bench at games, then there is one more step:
>
> Step 8 - Get your CEP Certification
> I recommend everyone do this step because 1) it makes you an official coach, 2) it is pretty easy compared to steps 1-6, and 3) you have until December 31, 2022. Clinics can be done on Zoom and are usually set up as two 3-4 hour classes two days apart, although there are many, many coaching seminars offered so there are definitely options including limited in person options. You can take ANY clinic with an open seat, just keep the time zone in mind if you sign up for courses outside the Eastern time zone. Yes, this is a decent chunk of additional time you have to invest but you can just sit there with your camera on and get credit for the class. Personally I thought mine was actually interesting because the seminar leader talked a lot about how to coach and how to deal with different situations, so it was not just going through what you already know from the age-specific modules.
>
> Keep in mind, what level of clinic you sign up for depends on what CEP level you currently hold (and has nothing to do with which age group you are coaching, your years of experience, etc). So if you have not yet been certified, you take Level 1. If you were Level 1 last year then you take Level 2 this year, etc.
>
> Link to sign up for CEP clinics: https://www.usahockey.com/coachingclinics
>
> NOTE: clinics are posted in bunches so be persistent if you are having difficulty getting into a clinic at a time that works for you. This might be annoying but it will work out. You have until December 31, 2022.
> Currently clinics are posted through October 15
> October 1 - clinics posted through November 15
> November 1 - clinics posted through December 15
> December 1 - clinics posted through December 31
> Certification MUST be complete by December 31, 2022.
