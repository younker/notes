A few notes:

1 – The Soo was by far the most physical team we’ve faced.

2 – From the 3rd period of the first game intothe second game, they dominated possession as we played afraid to get hit.  They are a sign of what we should expectgoing into Canada.

3 – 3 of the 7 goals they scored were from a feed frombehind the goal line.  They had manyother great opportunities too.

-         We largely gave up the space behind our own net andcorners and had many turnovers in the process of avoiding getting hit while retrievingthe puck from behind the goal line ([[Judah Cook|Judah]] was particularly guilty of this)

-         There is confusion between the center and defensemenwhen the Soo had a player behind the goal line as to who should attack thatplayer, when they did, and who should cover the front of the goal

4 – We frequently attempted breakouts from behind the net throughSoo players when the other side had a Petoskey player.  The defensemen off-the-puck needs to bemaking a call for a switch if the defensemen with the puck is surrounded andunable to make a good pass out.

5 – The ref that kicked Stephen out of the game could nothave seen what Stephen did and only lifted his arm once he saw the Soo kid hunchedover.  The same Soo kid, prior to that,cross-checked Stephen at the head in front of the same ref right at that veryface-off.
