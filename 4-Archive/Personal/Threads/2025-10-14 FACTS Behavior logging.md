## 2. 2025-10-15 13:05 — heatheryounker@outlook.com

*Subject: Re: FACTS Behavior logging*

Too much? Or just enough?

Thanks for the prompt reply, Kelly. Yes, I do have questions.

I have questions about how the information I FACTS is used.

Is it a tool the teachers use to come together alongside a student to help them improve during struggles that are seen from class to class?
Or is it a tool that is used to document behavior so the school can take action against the student?

Either way, the tool is only as good as the data that is recorded. If the staff is all following the same guidelines for every student, then I can see it as an excellent tool to determine what behavior is happening in the school and to see patterns for individual students. If the recording of incidents in FACTS is not used the same across the staff and for all students, it can paint a distorted view of what is happening across the student body. In this case, it is a tool in a broken system that inspires words like bias, partiality, favoritism. Which brings me to my next concern....

I have questions on consistency, both in when parents are being involved and when it is recorded in FACTS. We appreciate being able to talk with Emily about her behavior in class as it was not OK, but also given that disrespect is not something Mrs. Dawley nor her other teachers typically experience with Emily. Em said she was having an off week and immediately felt terrible - she likes and respects Mrs. Dawley very much and felt sad to disappoint her....and herself. However, the lack of consistency is troubling. Emily's behavior in class was minor compared to multiple other incidents of disobedience, disruptive behavior, and even mocking the teacher directly in front of her over the past several weeks in the same class where parents were not contacted. Additionally, in speaking with other parents it seems there is little clarity and consistency for when things are logged into FACTS. For example, a student with multiple severe behavior issues and who is on probation has never had anything recorded in FACTS according to his mother. We appreciate having an opportunity to parent our children through issues. We like being involved with the school and it's something that we value greatly at Harbor Light. We love that Emily is at a school that fosters an environment of partnering together to bring up the children according to His ways. However, I don't think any student or family wants to feel like they are under more scrutiny or having to walk a straighter line than others.

We are concerned given that two recent issues have been brought to our attention where incorrect conclusions were drawn (Emily eating ice cream for her lunch) or an incident was misrepresented to us in email (Emily calling Jason names for touching her pen). Emily is trying very hard to stay focused at school and to get good grades and be her best. No one is harder on Emily than Emily. She is feeling the pressure at school and honestly it's perplexing.

We would like to meet with you to discuss our concerns.

Thank you for listening. You have a difficult job and we appreciate all you do.
-Heather
