> It would be an honor to host your Alaskan fishing trip July 5-9, 2021. Nushagak River Adventures Lodge guests primarily choose our full-service lodge in Bristol Bay for our creature comforts like new, warm, dry cabins furnished with real beds and linens versus the tent camps on the river that offer cots and sleeping bags. You will sleep comfortably in two of our private cabins with four comfortable beds, several electrical outlets, bedding, and pillows.  We renovated the entire property since I bought it in January 2017. A brand-new main lodge and a brand-new bathhouse with hot showers and flush toilets separated for men and women are two more features that guests love instead of shower tents and outhouses found at the fish camps.  We are also selected for our outstanding guided fishing program and impressive “5-star in the bush” dining.
>
> Our fee is $3800 per angler. It would be ideal if there were two or more of you staying in the four person cabin.
>
> As you know, the Nushagak River is one the most productive and healthy salmon rivers in Bristol Bay with the world’s largest natural king salmon run. Our eight professional guides run new Alumaweld boats and motors with all new fishing gear. Your fully guided salmon fishing trip on the Nushagak is a great value. Your fully guided salmon fishing trip on the Nushagak is a great value in part because we offer so much more and we are the only outfitter open during the Coho season.
>
> The price per angler includes your roundtrip float plane ride to and from Dillingham Alaska, and all meals prepared by our Executive Chef including a plated 3-course dinner. A cozy cabin, fully guided fishing, and free Internet usage. It also includes fish preparation—vacuum sealed, frozen, boxed, placed in a fish box that holds roughly 50lbs, ready for your flight home.
>
> Here’s how our program works. You arrive Dillingham AK either the night prior and sleep at a B&B or hotel of your choice, or you arrive Dillingham the morning of your Day 1, prior to 9am, so that we can float plane you to the lodge. It is a 25-minute float plane ride to our island on the Nushagak. We are self-contained with electric power, septic, water, etc.
>
> Upon arrival, you will receive a 15-minute safety and orientation briefing, you’ll be provided your lunch, and you will be off to fish, returning around 6pm. At the end of each day there are appetizers and a 3-course delicious dinner prepared by our two executive chefs.
>
> Day 2 starts with breakfast 7-8am, then off to the boats with your lunch. You are welcome to return any time but otherwise, your guide will bring you back by 6pm. The same is true Day 3 and Day 4. On the morning of Day 5, you will depart by 10am back to Dillingham on the floats that bring the next wave of clients.
>
> Please refer to our FAQs at this link https://fishthenush.com/frequently-asked-questions/
>
> Please refer to this link for what is and is not included in your package. https://fishthenush.com/fishing-packages/
>
> For a first hand description of Nushagak River Adventures Lodge, read about us in the January 2019 issue of Fish Alaska magazine and more recently, the December 2020 issue of Fish Alaska Magazine found on our Blog webpage (https://fishthenush.com/blog/) along with other topics like “Flying to Alaska”, “Silver Fishing” and so many more. Our website holds more up-to-date information including videos and photo gallery; our frequently asked questions (FAQs), and a list of what’s included and not included in your stay.
>
> Should you agree this is the lodge for you, Ms. Jean Moore, CPA (jean@fishthenush.com) will be in touch to coordinate invoices and pass along other information. Please let us know if you have any questions.
>
> All you need to do is send me an email confirming you are ready to book. You’ll appreciate how easy it is to work with us and our timely communication. We’ve built many friendships with our clients and have a high repeat guest rate.
>
> In short, guests choose Nushagak River Adventures Lodge because:
>
> 1. Nushagak River salmon fishing is the best in Alaska.
> 2. Our guides are top notch and catch lots of fish!
> 3. The welcoming staff is pleasant, professional and safety conscious.
> 4. A remote and serene location gets you away from crowds and heavy fishing pressure.
> 5. Our lodging is heads and shoulders above the tent camps.
> 6. We serve 5-star cuisine that is truly memorable.
> 7. Our operation is run smoothly, with excellent business practices.
