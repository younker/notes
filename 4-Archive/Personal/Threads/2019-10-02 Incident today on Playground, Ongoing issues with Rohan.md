# Incident today on Playground, Ongoing issues with Rohan

**3 messages** from 2019-10-02 to 2019-10-03

## 1. 2019-10-02 00:30 — heathergoffinet@hotmail.com

Hi Tricia,

I think it is important that you are aware of an incident that occurred today during recess. As you know I am now present on the playground during recess to ensure Stephen and Emily are safe due to the previous threats and aggressive behavior from Rohan towards Stephen specifically. While on recess today, a group of kids, including Emily and Stephen, were gathering the gumballs? under the gumball? tree and collecting them as part of some imaginative play. Rohan, Atticus and Argus were up by the bike racks. They made their way down to the group who was gathering the gumballs, Atticus and Argus came to look over Stephen and the others at what they were gathering. Rohan stopped approximately 4 feet from Stephen. I noticed there was something in his hand. I was approximately 15 feet from him. From where I was standing it looked like a 5-6 inch object with a sharp point that he was sharpening on a rock. Susan was nearby at this point as well. Rohan looked up and saw Susan and proceeded to stick the object in his pocket and start to walk away. Susan stopped him and spoke to him briefly and then she started walking back closer to where I was standing. I was concerned that he might be concealing something dangerous given his previous threats, so I asked her if she checked the sharp object that he was holding. She replied that she did not. I told her that I saw him with something sharp that he quickly stuck in his pocket when she approached. I asked if she could check since there are safety concerns right now with Rohan. She said she would though her body language made it clear to me that she was unhappy to do so. She went to talk to Rohan, he pulled some small items from his pocket to show her but did not show her the item I had seen him carrying moments before. Susan came back and said, "It's just a sharks tooth - it's a treasure." I let her know that he did not show her the object I was concerned about. She kept walking away from me and said that there was nothing there. I pressed the issue since there are safety concerns and let her know that I felt it important that she recheck since Rohan did threaten to bring a knife to school and kill Stephen to which she rolled her eyes and said, "Oh, Rohan isn't going to bring a  knife to school and Rohan isn't going to kill Stephen. But ok, I'll check." She then went back over to Rohan and talked to him, he emptied several other small items from his pocket but again did not immediately reveal the object of concern. After what looked like a little back and forth from Susan, he did finally show the object to her. She walked back up towards me and told me, "It was a pencil." I thanked her for checking. Recess then ended.

The situation of Rohan having something sharp on recess and then coming near Stephen and then making an effort to conceal the object, as well as, it taking multiple requests/attempts for him to reveal it, is upsetting and concerning. If there was no ill will intended, why hide the pencil? Why pull every other little pebble, shark teeth and other objects out but not reveal it? And why was he sharpening it on a stone? The situation is alarming, however, Susan's response to the concern today highlights a second issue, which is whether or not the school is taking our children's safety seriously. It's confusing why the context around and the physical harm done to Stephen prior to Rohan threatening to 'bring a knife to school and finish him off' was not relayed to Susan so she understands the severity of the issue. I understand that Susan is quicker to dismiss issues on the playground than most adults at school, but I would like to think that if she had that info perhaps she would have taken the situation today more seriously. Maybe not. Regardless, we're left feeling unsupported  by our school during a time when we should have an abundance of support to ensure our children's safety.

After recess, I was upset about Susan's response and went to the Resource Room to talk to her about it. I approached the conversation very calmly and politely - despite feeling upset, I absolutely did not want the conversation to be tense or to leave any ill will in a relationship that has historically been a good one. I let her know that I wanted to talk to her about what happened at recess. I told her that I was not trying to inflame the situation or create discomfort but given the safety concerns we have with Rohan, I thought it was imperative to ensure he was not concealing anything dangerous. I let her know that her response to my request was upsetting and did not indicate to me that she was taking the safety issue seriously. She responded by saying that kids say things they don't mean all the time and she hasn't seen any concerning behavior between the boys. I let her know that had the threat been a one time situation, I would not be present for all recesses and I would not have escalated the issue for support. I explained to her that Rohan's behavior has been an issue we have been dealing with for over a year and we only sought additional support when Rohan's behavior - both physical harm and verbal threats increased. She reiterated that she doesn't see anything concerning and that usually if a threat is a concern it is accompanied with escalating behavior such as additional words or actions. I told her that Rohan threatened to bring a knife to school and kill Stephen after choking him from behind on Sept 12th and that this was not the first time Rohan has threatened to kill Stephen (referencing the incident on May 28th - brought to the school's attention on Sat, June 1st). She replied that she was not aware of that. I told her that there is probably a lot about the issue that she was not aware of and I asked that she just understand that I would not be there at school every day during recess if the situation and the kids safety was not a valid concern. She went on to say that she was not concerned from what she saw happening. I let her know that I understand that from her reaction but Jason and I are concerned about the situation and that you had met with us regarding the issue and you seem concerned about it as well. She then went on to say that she felt bad for persecuting Rohan and insisting he turn his pockets out without any reason for doing so and that she wasn't there to police the kids. I reminded her that the reason to check on the sharp object was a direct result of Rohan threatening to bring a sharp object to school and kill my son. She then went on to say that perhaps keeping Stephen home during lunch would be a good option. I felt the whole tone of the conversation started with her being dismissive, then moved to being defensive. I felt like we, as the victims, in this situation were being victimized again. We continued to talk and I intend to do a comprehensive documentation from my memory while it is fresh after sending this email to you. My priority is notifying you first. During the conversation she remained typing on her computer and only gave me eye contact after I stood for a long while waiting for her to give it. Susan and I have always had a pleasant relationship and I do not want to upset any of the good relationships we have at the school, but I left that discussion feeling disappointment with the lack of concern and feeling like the school is just not taking this issue seriously at all.

Since our meeting on Sept. 17th, I have been present at lunch and recess (last Thursday was the first day I started reading on the bench outside of lunch and then stay with the kids during recess). In this time, I've observed behavior from Rohan that is concerning given the current situation and climate. Our message to the kids is that they are to avoid Rohan at recess and just play and enjoy their time with friends. By my observation, the kids are following this 100%.


  *   Sept 17 - Stephen played on the bars and ran around with a group of friends, including Rahul, Rohan's little brother who they have frequently played with without issue for the past year. According to the kids after school, during this time Rahul said he felt bad about what Rohan had said and did and said he still wanted to play together. They played for awhile without issue (there has never been an issue with Emily, Stephen and Rahul playing as a group).  Rohan walked around the playground for awhile and then after 10 minutes or so seemed to notice Rahul, came over to him to talk to him and pulled him by the arm away from the group.
  *   Sept 19 - Stephen joined what looked like nearly all of the Mosaic kids in a game of freeze tag. There was a big group playing. When I walked from lunch to recess the game was already in session. Stephen was "frozen" down on one knee when I made it to the playground. Rohan, Argus and Atticus were crouched down behind him about 6-8 feet behind Stephen. Rohan was whispering things to Argus who was staring straight ahead (for the record, Argus has always been very kind to Stephen and we have not seen nor do we have concerns with Argus nor Atticus). While Rohan was whispering to Argus, Argus was watching the game on not appearing to engage in the conversation. After awhile, Atticus joined the freeze tag game and Rohan went off to walk by himself, stopping at the gumball tree to pluck a gumball as well as a 4-5" curved stick with a point from the tree, put them in his pocket and walk back closer to the game.
  *   Sept 24 - we were home sick
  *   Sept 26 - Stephen played Freeze Tag again with a group of friends. I did not see him even glance at Rohan as he was very submersed in the game. Rohan, Argus and Atticus were walking around the perimeter of the game. At one point, Stephen was "frozen" in the game and down on one knee (which is part of the game and the signal to the other kids that they need unfrozen). He was facing the bars which was also facing me. Rohan was walking towards Stephen along the trees, also the perimeter of the game. Rohan and Atticus both made shooting motions towards the game with their hands. (again, we haven't had any prior incidents with Atticus). They then stopped about 10 feet behind where Stephen was crouched down and were talking. Susan went to talk to the 3 boys and I am not aware of whether or not she was addressing anything related to this issue. Of course, the shooting motions with their hands towards Stephen could all be a coincidence but viewing it through the lens of the current safety concern, it is concerning. Towards the end of recess, while Stephen was frozen a separate time, I saw Rahul come up to Stephen and was yelling at him. The recess whistle then sounded and Stephen came up to me visibly upset. He said that Rahul said, "We are banned from speaking to any of you. And this is the last word I say to you - you're not my friend anymore." Stephen had fun at recess up to this point but left recess quite upset. I respect that Rahul wanted to set a boundary and that the situation must be confusing for him. Stephen is very sad to lose Rahul as a friend, but we explained to him that this might happen given what is happening with Rohan. However, it was hard to see Stephen's joyful recess time interrupted in this manner and only continues the pattern of the McConnaha boys being the ones to engage Stephen.
  *   Oct 1 - incident outlined above.

Obviously, I am documenting everything. From my observation, Stephen is doing exactly as Jason and I have instructed. He is keeping his distance and I haven't even seen Stephen look at Rohan (with the exception of today when Rohan was standing near Stephen with the sharp object and he said he accidentally locked eyes with Rohan once today during lunch). What I am viewing from Rohan however is that he is very aware of Stephen and not proceeding to disregard Stephen and enjoy himself at recess. Rohan's behavior from the lens of past concerning behavior appears to be him posturing or attempting to intimidate Stephen. I asked Susan today if she noticed Rohan's recess behavior and she said that she has not, other than seeing him walking around the edge of the game and stopping (as referenced above on Sept 26). I was disappointed that she hadn't noticed the behavior that I am seeing, but then if she doesn't acknowledge that a problem exists, she would not be looking at the situation from the same lens that we are viewing it.

I do not know the path forward here. We are trying to handle the situation as best we can and I feel we are tiptoeing around the school in order to keep pleasant relationships, while still trying to do everything we can to ensure the safety of our kids. Ultimately, nothing is more important that our children's safety - BISD commits the same. I did see in the newsletter today that there is a specific form from the Handbook for which to report bullying issues and safety concerns. I stumbled upon it at random but was happy to have found a process of documentation. I intend to fill this out and submit it to you this week so that there is a written record of the incidents thus far.

The kids love Mosaic - they enjoy their classes and have fun with their friends. It is so frustrating to see the experience tainted by one kid who persists in his unfounded hatred of Stephen. Of course, pulling the kids out is an option but it just doesn't seem fair to take Mosaic away from them, especially when they are the victims in this situation. So what are our options?

Jason and I think another meeting is probably appropriate to discuss this issue again and how to proceed. Please let us know your thoughts.

Thank you for your time and continued efforts.
-Heather Younker
