Good evening, Jason and Heather,

I wanted to take a moment to follow up on the corn maze incident from Friday night.

First, I want to express how sorry I am that what was otherwise a fun, memory-filled evening had this unfortunate disruption. My hope is that, together, we can use this as an opportunity for growth.

After speaking with Stephen and hearing accounts from the other boys and a few young ladies nearby, I’d like to share my best understanding of what took place, along with some thoughts on how we can move forward.

It seems that what started as innocent play between Stephen and the other boys gradually escalated into roughhousing, and from there, into actual fighting. Based on the reports I’ve received, the students involved were Stephen (7th grade), Crew (5th grade, homeschooled with siblings at HLCS), Peyton (5th grade), James (4th grade), and James’s cousin (2nd grade).

The boys were all chasing each other in a game of tag, and at some point, Peyton tripped. His friends assumed Stephen had tripped him, and it was overheard that Crew said, “let’s get him.” At this point, Crew reportedly punched Stephen in the private area twice. The situation escalated into a physical back-and-forth, with both boys wrestling and pushing each other. Stephen and Crew both came out of the maze limping and sore, with grass stains on their clothes.

>From what I’ve gathered, Stephen didn’t punch anyone, but it does sound like both boys were equally aggressive in their responses to one another. While I can’t be certain of every detail, I wanted to share the information I’ve received.

While incidents like these are always unfortunate, they can be valuable moments to learn, reflect, and move forward in a positive direction.

Looking ahead, on the school’s side, if we return to Uncle Frank’s corn maze next year, we plan to have two adult chaperones inside to help prevent any future situations from escalating.

Because this was a family event, and since Crew is not enrolled at HLCS, I don’t feel it’s appropriate to issue any formal consequences through the school.

God has entrusted you with Stephen, and you love and support him so well. I encourage you to take a few steps with him if you haven’t already. First, I’d suggest sitting down with him to talk about the importance of appropriate play, especially in physical situations. It may also be helpful to pray with him, focusing on forgiveness and self-control when play begins to get rough. Additionally, as an older student, it could be valuable to discuss how he can set a positive example when playing with younger children.

Second, I encourage you to reach out to Crew’s family, the Goldsmiths, to seek reconciliation. At Harbor Light, we are a close-knit community, and as with any community, misunderstandings and conflicts can arise. Healing happens best when issues are addressed head-on, rather than ignored. Please let me know if I can assist in helping both families connect.

Thank you so much for your time. If you have any questions or additional insights, I’d love to hear them as we work together to navigate this situation.

In Christ,
Kelly
