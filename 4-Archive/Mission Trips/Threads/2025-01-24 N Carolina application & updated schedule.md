Hello Friends,
Thank you for your interest in the North Carolina Trip! If you have not
already filled out your *application
<https://genesispetoskey.ccbchurch.com/goto/forms/208/responses/new>*

check, <https://genesispetoskey.ccbchurch.com/goto/forms/20/responses/new>*
please do so soon! *APPLICATIONS ARE DUE JANUARY 13! *

Based on feedback from our first informational meeting, we have made some
changes to the schedule. The updated schedule reflects increased work time
and the elimination of sightseeing on the way home. We will incorporate
some local outings in the evenings after dinner. Please read the details
below and reach out with any questions.

Thanks,
Andrea

*Basic Info:*

   - *Dates:* March 22-29, 2025
   - *Tentative Schedule: *
   - Saturday, March 22 am depart Petoskey,* sleep at hotel*
      - Sunday, March 23: arrive at Excel College (between 2-5pm). Dinner
      6pm, orientation
      - Monday-Thurs: workdays 9am- 4pm, dinner 5pm, Evening team
      time/outings TBD
      - Friday, March 28: work am, depart 2pm—*sleep at hotel*
      - Saturday, March 29: Drive to Petoskey
      - *Connection:* We will be working in collaboration with Excel
   College in North Carolina. Several people from Genesis have strong ties to
   Excel College, and we are excited to partner with them in this ministry!
   Excel College has formed a coalition with Adventures Relief, Operation
   Blessing, and World Vision to provide relief and rebuild homes. Read more
   on their website. *https://www.theexcelcollege.com/disaster-relief
   <https://www.theexcelcollege.com/disaster-relief>*
   - *Travel:* Individual vehicles for those who choose to drive, and bus
   or van rental for those not taking their own vehicle.
   - *Lodging/Facilities in NC: *
   - Bunk rooms, 30 bunks per room
      - Heated tents available, 20 people
      - For those who want more privacy, there is Ridgecrest Motel, one
      mile up the road. The cost is $100 for a room with two double beds.
      - There is a tractor/trailer with bathrooms and showers, also a
      shower trailer with separate men’s/women’s sides. Very nice condition!
      - They are working on building a large meeting space for shared
      meals.
      - Operation Blessing will be doing all of the cooking. They CAN
      accommodate gluten free diets and have a variety of foods available.
      - *Who Can Help:* The greatest need is for skilled construction
   workers. We need one construction team lead for every 5-8 people. This is
   someone who is skilled AND can teach others. Construction team leads will
   meet in the mornings and evenings with Adventures in Missions to learn
   about the day’s project and also to debrief/plan for the next day.
   - *Can I help if I am not a skilled construction worker? *Yes! You can
   be part of a team, or you could help in other local ministry such as a
   local food pantry: organizing, distributing and sorting donations, family
   support, general cleanup or other areas of service.
   - *Cost: *$400 per person.  *** Financial assistance is available.
   Please do not let this deter you!***
   - *Deadline: *1st payment ($100) due Feb 15, 2nd payment due March 15
      - *What’s included:* 2 nights at the hotel plus dinners, 5 nights at
      Excel College & all meals while at Excel College, round-trip
      transportation.
      - *What’s not included:* Snacks and lunch while on the road, spending
      money. Supplies: work gloves, bedding, journal, etc. See handout for full
      packing list.
      - *What if I drive myself?* If you are willing to drive your vehicle,
      we will discount your trip by $100 per person that you transport.  For
      example, if you drive yourself and your spouse plus three additional
      people, we will credit you $500.
