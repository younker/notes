## Developer Growth

* What does “developer growth” mean to you and what is your basic approach to
  promoting it?

  **answer**
  - recognized different people enjoy/excel at different things and focus your
    efforts on strengthening those skills while ensuring a certain amount of
    competency across the board
  $ did not mention attitude or specific techniques


* What is your approach to working with employees who do not intend to grow but
  rather just want to do their job, collect a paycheck and go home

  **answer**
  - that is fine (for now) but as the product/team grows and they stagnate, it's
    time to grow or leave
  - people cannot be complacent.

* What is they try to grow but do not (can not)

  **answer**
  - if they cannot, then identify what they can do and lead them in that direction
  $ there is empathy in his answer which is nice. An attempt to work with the
    employee in order to help them succeed
  - Provided anecdote about a product person moving to dev and it didn't work.
    The employee then made a choice to move back. If they had not, then it might
    not be a good fit for the company or the person, and is ok to part ways

* What are some proactive steps you can take to initiate or facilitate mentoring
  on your team?

  **answer**
  - help the employee to ask the right questions (?)
  - pair the employee with a senior dev
  - help the employee navigate how to get there (?)
  - be mentored by a senior dev
  - give them more responsibility & ownership
  - put them into a position to grow
  - put into a FT dev lead position

* How do you overcome a situation where a team finds itself without any capable
  or willing mentors?

  **answer**
  $ chiaryn asked this in her own words
  - if no mentors exist, take the initiative and teach. Inform the group of your
    standards, practicies, etc and in doing stuff you will grow

* How would you approach a situation where an employee wants to people manage
  without giving up all of their day-to-day software development?

  **answer**
  - why do they want to be a manager? The reason is important
  - perhaps a (feature team) lead position would allow them to both contribute
    and serve
  - managing is growing individuals, product & leading by serving the company
    and team
  - in the past he has been part of a server/leader || player/coach model which
    worked well

* diversity...

  **answer**
  - talked about varying skill sets and levels of achievement
  - integrating women engineers into a team, spoke of womens leadership, workshops, etc
  - spoke to cultural diversity. respecting holidays, traditions, cultural norms

* How do you ensure diversity in your recruiting pipeline

  **answer**
  I heard little about cultural/gender diversity and mostly interest in getting
  the most qualified individual. 

* how do you work with an employee who reports discrimination

  **answer**
  described a scenario where a female was reporting discrimination among
  a male dominated team -- get HR involved

  described a scenario where he made a comment (seemingly out of empathy)
  which was hurtful to the employee. He recognized it and addressed it with
  her the following day
