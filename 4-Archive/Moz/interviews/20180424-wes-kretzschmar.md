
To be honest, my first impression of Wes was one of self importance. A candidate
wants to project confidence and we certainly want our employees to be confident
but for much of the interview I tried to understand if this was confidence,
arrogance or both and what that meant for the culture we want here at Moz...but
I'll come back to this later.

Wes provided many anecdotes, one of the highlights being a situation around
gender pay equality in which a female direct was being paid far less than her
male peers. After escalating the issue, he was able to procur an additional pay
bump despite HR guidelines prohibiting additional pay raises outside of their
annual review (however, that still did fully remedy the problem).

Another striking anecdote involved a developer who was struggling with
aggression aimed towards his peers and was on the verge of being fired. Wes
approached the situation with (tough) honestly and empathy striving to
understand where this invidiual was coming from and why he was acting the way he
was. His approach seems to have paid off as the individual is still with the
company and acting in a lead capacity as scrum master (which plays to his
strengths).

These 2 anecdotes, if true and accurate, address the question around arrogance
vs confidence. His confidence allowed him to approach an otherwise intimidating
employeee as well as buck a large company standard around pay for the sake of
what is right. These actions demonstrate to me enough competence and adherence
to TAGFEE principles.

He spoke of a manager's role as one of guidance, ensuring growth, ensuring team
dynamics, removing roadblocks & being an advocate for your directs. He also
mentioned how proper scrum addresses many traditional manager roles which I
thought was both clever and true. In fact, as he spoke about agile development I
found myself wanting to integrate more strict scrum principles into our
development cycle.

Most everything he said was solid and reading the above you might think I am a
firm yes. However, there is a part of me, a gut feeling perhaps, that cautions
against Wes. I cannot articulate it or really even put much of a finger on it,
but I have a hesitation saying Yes. However, because I do not have a reason to
say no and many to say yes, I am a soft yes and look forward to others feedback!
