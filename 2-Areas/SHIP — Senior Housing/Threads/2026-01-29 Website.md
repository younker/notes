Here's some info to give the web guy. Take a look and let me know what you think.

Website: www.carepathiq.com<http://www.carepathiq.com> or www.searchaiq.com<http://www.searchaiq.com>

Website Comparison: www.aplaceformom.com<http://www.aplaceformom.com> "APFM"

The agent/website: make it a structured wizard (similar to APFM that produces a list of high probability matches)
Inputs

  1.  City or ZIP and radius
  2.  Timeline and urgency
  3.  ADL support needs (Bathing, Dressing, Eating, Medication Management, etc.)
  4.  Memory and safety concerns (wandering, falls, medication)
  5.  Budget range and who is paying
  6.  Preferences: pet, couples, culture, diet, activities, faith, language
Output

  1.  "Likely level of care" with "why this" and "when to reassess"
  2.  A shortlist generated from your directory filtered by hard constraints (location, care type, budget)
  3.  A "questions to ask" list tailored to their case
  4.  A "red flags" list tailored to their case
  5.  A next step plan (tour order, documents to gather, who to involve)

Ranking logic (keep it transparent) Use an explainable scoring model that you publish at a high level.

Safety escalation If the user indicates imminent danger, severe wandering, recent hospitalization discharge with high needs, suicidal ideation, or abuse concerns, the agent should stop and provide urgent resources and "seek professional assessment" guidance.
