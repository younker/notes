# Summary: Senior Housing Facility Selection - LLM vs Traditional Data Service

**1 message** on 2025-12-30

## 1. 2025-12-30 11:49 — jason@ynkr.org

Hi Jason,

Here's a summary of our discussion about building a senior housing facility selection tool:

PROJECT OVERVIEW
A system to help people choose senior housing facilities for loved ones based on multiple variables including cost, facility violations history, location, and amenities. The goal is to calculate both a base score for facilities and customized scores for individuals.

RECOMMENDATION: Traditional Data Service (Not LLM-Based)

A traditional data service is the better primary solution because:

• Structured Decision-Making: You have defined, quantifiable factors that databases and algorithms handle excellently
• Accuracy & Consistency: High-stakes decisions require deterministic, reliable results that don't vary
• Transparency: Traditional scoring algorithms are auditable and you can clearly explain why a facility scored the way it did
• Data Structure: Facility data is naturally structured (databases/spreadsheets)

WHERE AN LLM COULD ADD VALUE (As a Complementary Layer):

• Natural language search interface ("Find facilities within 10 miles of Boston under $5k/month with memory care")
• Summarizing reviews or inspection reports in plain language
• Answering user questions about specific facilities
• Helping users understand which factors to prioritize

RECOMMENDED APPROACH:

1. Build a traditional data service with:
- Database of facilities with structured data
- Clear, transparent scoring algorithm
- Search and filtering capabilities

2. Optionally add an LLM layer on top for natural language interaction and explanations

This approach gives you reliability where it matters most while still leveraging AI for user experience improvements.
