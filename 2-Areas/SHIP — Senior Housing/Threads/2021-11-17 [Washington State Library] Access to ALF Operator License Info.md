Hello Jason,

Kathleen Sullivan with the Open Data Portal forwarded this question to the reference librarians here at the Washington State Library.

So far I have not been able to find a way to search online for operator license details or applications for ALFs in Washington.

Though it's not licensing applications, a Corporation Search by facility name or Uniform Business Identification (UBI) number from the Secretary of State's Corporations search https://ccfs.sos.wa.gov/#/ does give you some information on ownership of a facility.

You can also see any documents filed with the Secretary of State such as registrations, annual reports, and name changes, etc. by clicking on the Filing History button at the bottom of the business information page. I have attached an example of the kind of information available through the Corporations page.

DSHS has a FAQ for anyone with licensing questions who wants to open an ALF: https://www.dshs.wa.gov/altsa/faq?field_altsa_topics_value=alf.

I recommend emailing the DSHS Business Analysis and Application Unit at baau@dshs.wa.gov <http://dshs.wa.gov> to ask if ALF licensing applications are public record and if so whether they can be found online.

I suspect you may have to submit a public records request, assuming the applications are public record.

For more detail on a Public Record Request see https://www.dshs.wa.gov/office-of-the-secretary/how-request-public-records.



Sincerely,

Jason,

I'm following up on my colleague's thorough reply to you from last week. I wondered if this information had been opened somewhere, so I emailed the Business Analysis and Application Unit, as Kathryn suggested. As she expected, this would indeed involve a public records request, and possibly some discussion with the Public Disclosure Unit.

Attached is a copy of the Request for Records form. You can also contact the Public Disclosure Unit at pdd@dshs.wa.gov <mailto:pdd@dshs.wa.gov>.

Entire applications are apparently available on request, except for any content not subject to disclosure under the Public Records Act. Any redactions and the reasons behind them would be documented by the agency.

So, no API or online repository, but another option. Please reach out if we can help you more.

Kathleen Sullivan

Jason,

1) In this case "application" does not mean an online tool or API.

It means the information that an ALF had to provide to DSHS to apply for a license. When you "apply" for a license you have to fill out an "application." It has nothing to do with the programmer definition of applications or "apps."

2)Yes, contact a DSHS public records officer with any other questions about requesting information on multiple ALFs.



Sincerely,
