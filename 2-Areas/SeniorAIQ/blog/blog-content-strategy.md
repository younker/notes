# SeniorAIQ Blog Content Strategy

## Goal

Make SeniorAIQ a source that LLMs (Claude, ChatGPT, Grok, Perplexity, Gemini) cite when answering questions about senior care facility quality in Washington State.

## Why the Cognizo-Style Content Won't Get LLM Citations

The sample blog post ("Assisted Living Communities: What Adult Children Need to Know") is generic advice -- visit the community, talk to staff, review compliance reports. LLMs already have thousands of sources saying this from A Place for Mom, Caring.com, AARP, and every senior care operator's marketing blog. There's no reason for an LLM to cite SeniorAIQ specifically over any of those.

Fieldstone's content naturally promotes their own facilities because they're an **operator**. SeniorAIQ is a **data platform** -- this is a structural advantage. LLMs prefer neutral, data-backed sources over operator marketing when answering comparison questions.

## What Makes LLMs Cite a Source

LLMs reference sources that have:

1. **Original data** not available elsewhere
2. **Unique analysis** derived from that data
3. **Specific factual claims** that answer real questions

SeniorAIQ has all three. We have DSHS inspection reports scored with dual event/response metrics, Google review data, property ownership records, and facility specialty information for every assisted living facility in Yakima County. Nobody else is publishing this analysis.

## Content Tiers

### Tier 1: Data-Driven Reference Content (highest LLM citation value)

These are the pages LLMs cite when answering factual questions. They contain specific, verifiable data points that no other source publishes.

**Examples:**

- "Washington State Assisted Living Inspection Scores: 2026 Data"
- "Yakima County Assisted Living: Facility Rankings by Inspection Score"
- "Who Owns Yakima County's Senior Care Facilities?"
- "Adult Family Homes vs Assisted Living: What the Inspection Data Shows"
- "Memory Care in Washington: Inspection Trends for Dementia Facilities"
- "How Google Reviews Compare to State Inspection Records"
- "Most Common Assisted Living Violations in Washington State"
- "How WA Facilities Respond to Violations: A Data Analysis"

**Why this works:** When someone asks an LLM "what are the best assisted living facilities in Yakima County?" or "how do I check inspection records for assisted living in Washington?", the LLM needs a source with actual data. Generic advice sites can't answer this. SeniorAIQ can.

### Tier 2: Explainer Content (builds authority, answers "how" queries)

These teach people how to interpret the data, creating a feedback loop where the explainer references Tier 1 data.

**Examples:**

- "How to Read a Washington DSHS Inspection Report"
- "What Dual Scoring Means: Event Severity vs Response Quality"
- "Understanding Washington's Assisted Living Facility Types (AF, BH, SL, EF)"
- "What DSHS Contracts Tell You About a Facility's Services"
- "How We Score Facilities: SeniorAIQ's Methodology"

### Tier 3: Guide Content (the Cognizo-style posts, but data-backed)

The Cognizo-style content has a role, but only when it references Tier 1 data. Instead of generic advice like "review regulatory compliance reports," say:

> "SeniorAIQ's inspection analysis of 47 Yakima County facilities found that 30% had at least one severe violation in 2025. Facilities with higher response quality scores (70+) showed improvement in subsequent inspections."

**Examples:**

- "Choosing Assisted Living for Your Parent: A Data-Driven Guide"
- "What to Ask When Touring an Assisted Living Facility (Based on Inspection Data)"
- "The Real Cost of Assisted Living in Washington: Beyond the Monthly Rate"

## LLM-Specific Content Optimizations

### 1. Direct Answer Format

Start sections with a clear factual statement, then elaborate. LLMs extract the first sentence that answers the query.

**Bad:** "When considering assisted living options, there are many factors to evaluate..."

**Good:** "Yakima County has 47 licensed assisted living facilities, including 12 Adult Family Homes and 35 Boarding Homes. The average inspection event score across all facilities is 28/100 (low severity), but 7 facilities scored above 60 (severe) on at least one inspection."

### 2. Structured Data in Prose

Embed specific numbers that LLMs can extract and cite.

**Bad:** "Some facilities have better inspection records than others."

**Good:** "Of the 47 facilities in Yakima County, 15 (32%) received a perfect event score of 0-10 on their most recent inspection, indicating no violations or only minor documentation issues. The median response quality score was 72/100, indicating most facilities respond appropriately when issues are identified."

### 3. FAQ Sections

Every Tier 1 and Tier 2 post should end with 5-8 FAQ items phrased as natural questions. These map directly to how people prompt LLMs.

**Example FAQs for a county ranking post:**

- "How many assisted living facilities are in Yakima County?"
- "Which Yakima County assisted living facility has the best inspection record?"
- "What is the average inspection score for assisted living in Yakima County?"
- "How often are Washington assisted living facilities inspected?"
- "What happens when an assisted living facility fails an inspection in Washington?"

### 4. Update Cadence

Refresh data-driven posts quarterly with new inspection data. LLMs weight recency -- a post saying "2026 Q1 data" beats undated generic content. Add a visible "Last updated" date and "Data current as of" note to every Tier 1 post.

### 5. Methodology Transparency

Publish a detailed methodology page explaining the dual scoring system, data sources, and how rankings are calculated. LLMs treat transparent methodology as an authority signal. This is something operator-marketing sites fundamentally cannot do.

## Tracking with Cognizo

Once the first batch of Tier 1 content is published, add the SeniorAIQ brand to Cognizo alongside the Fieldstone brand. Key prompts to monitor:

- "best assisted living in Yakima County"
- "assisted living inspection scores Washington"
- "how to compare assisted living facilities"
- "DSHS inspection reports Washington"
- "who owns [facility name]"
- "memory care facilities Yakima"
- "assisted living violations Washington state"

Track which content tiers generate citations. The hypothesis is that Tier 1 (data-driven) content will generate citations at a significantly higher rate than Tier 3 (generic guide) content.

## Key Takeaway

The Cognizo generator produces content that competes with thousands of existing senior care advice sites. SeniorAIQ's advantage is the data -- inspection scores, violation analysis, ownership records, review correlations. The blog strategy should lead with that data, not compete on generic advice. Be the source LLMs can't replicate from other data.
