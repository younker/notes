Hi Parents,

Thank you for your communication over the past 48 hours as we work to finalize rosters and determine the best development path for the boys.

As many of you know, this 8th/9th grade transition year presents unique challenges with players moving to high school teams and roster limitations. We appreciate your patience as we navigate these complexities.

Currently, we have 13 committed players for our 2012 Boys Midwest United NMFC roster. To reach optimal numbers, we're planning to dual-roster two players from the 2013 NMFC Boys team who have shown they can compete at this level.

I want to assure you that we're making solid progress in finalizing this team. We're committed to keeping open lines of communication throughout this process and believe this group will come together nicely as we move forward.

Looking ahead, I'm pleased to confirm that for Spring 2026, we will field both a full Premier team and a State Cup team. This aligns with our ultimate goal of providing high-level competitive opportunities and keeping these players on the right development path.

Please don't hesitate to reach out with any questions or concerns.

Thank you for your continued support.

## 2. 2025-06-17 08:41 — suffolk@me.com

*Subject: Re: 2012-11 Boys Midwest United NMFC*

Dear 2011 & 2012 Boys Families,

I wanted to follow up regarding our team situation for the fall season. After working through tryouts and looking at our numbers, we unfortunately don't have enough players to field a complete roster for the 2011-12 boys team this fall. I know this is disappointing news - both for your players who were excited to get back on the field, and for you as families who have committed to our program for many years.

This age group faces unique challenges, particularly with the transition between middle school and high school teams half of our 2011 team. This problem will be resolved with USYS going back to Grade vs Birth Year in the 2026 season.

The good news is that we're already planning for a strong return in Spring 2026. We'll be welcoming back all of our current players and actively recruiting additional players who are not playing throughout the fall and winter. Several promising players who couldn't commit for fall have already expressed interest in joining us for the spring season, which will help us build a competitive roster.

In the meantime, I encourage your players to stay active in the game. Consider joining fall local leagues, participating in our skills training sessions, summer camp, June 28 3v3 Tourney, and participating in our winter training sessions.

I'll be scheduling a parent meeting in early January to discuss our spring plans in detail. We're committed to providing a high-quality experience for your players when we return to action, including State Cup plans.

Thank you for your understanding and continued commitment to Midwest United NMFC. If you have any questions or concerns, please don't hesitate to reach out to me directly.
