Here are 3 variations of what can happen with that throw in (I’m sure as we are teaching this I’ll think of more)

Void: x3 moves into the void created by x2 checking back to the x1. Great because it’s simple and can move us forward quickly. Problem is that x3 might be moving into heavy traffic (that space is usually congested)

Switch: Great because we can switch the field quickly with this which is often a good play given everyone is settled in close to the throw in

Hand Off: The idea is to “hand off” x2’s defenseman (o1) to x1 freeing up x2 and making o1 look stupid because he is ball chasing. x2 has to "open up” to x1 otherwise pass is to his back
