Here is the promised breakdown of PYSA kids. I only count like ten 2012s so
they are going to need 2013s to round out the team, but I don't really know
who that might be. I numbered the kids on the photo and in the description
below.

Kids You Should Already Know
NMFC - Stephen, [[Roland|Roland]], [[Gavin|Gavin]] Jeffers, Grayson Baas

24-25 Lakers returning for 25-26
1) Grayson Broughman - Harbor Springs kid. Plays outside mid, usually on
the left but not always. Strong runner and passer. Pretty good player with
a lot of potential.

2) Theo Retherford - Petoskey kid. His dad Paul is on PYSA board. When
Suffolk took David's whole team and left for NMFC, Theo was the only kid
that stayed and has played up with the 2011s ever since. Solid with the
ball at his feet but a slow runner. Played right fullback and some midfield
in limited minutes this season.

3) [[Carter|Carter]] Strong - Petoskey. Was part of Suffolk's core group until a big
falling out over Jeff taking the MLS Next kids and leaving in the middle of
a tournament, abandoning Carter and the rest of the squad. His mom Carey is
a tough cookie and wasn't having it, so they quit NMFC between fall and
spring seasons so he has been back at PYSA half a year longer than David.
Carter is a right footed center back and has never been subbed for at least
5+ years across multiple coaches. Very solid player to build our team
around.

4) David Bolin - Petoskey. Plays outside mid, strong left foot but very
good with his right too. Confidence player so he can be up and down. Builds
into games so short shifts make it tough for him to build momentum. Needs
to be more confident running at defenders with the ball, and being more
aggressive on 50-50 balls. Despite often playing outside mid over the
years, his coaches haven't always been clear about when to get forward vs.
defending so sometimes is indecisive. Clarity from the coach on how to
approach that would really help.

5) [[Graham|Graham]] Wong - Petoskey. Baseball player who is relatively new to soccer,
but he is a freak athlete and has the craziest motor I have ever seen on a
kid. He nevers stops running the entire game and is rarely subbed. Played
the 6/CDM in front of a back 4, but his work rate is so high he basically
the 8/CAM at the same time. It is absolutely breathtaking watching this kid
vacuum up balls all over the field and then run straight up the field to
attack. Carter and Graham in the middle of the defensive unit is a heck of
a spine for this team.

6) [[Aiden|Aiden]] Giffin - Petoskey. His dad Jake is the Petoskey High School
building trades teacher, and I'm told they are very good family friends
with Stephen's girlfriend's family so you guys may have even met. We are
good friends with the Giffins. Aiden has exceptional footwork but almost
always hangs onto the ball too long. Plays outside mid, usually on the
right. Can be frustrating because of overhandling the ball and maybe not
always running as hard as he can off the ball. I believe he has also
suffered like David from a lack of clear coaching on how to play outside
mid. ADHD kid so not always as focused as you would want him to be at
practice, etc.

NOTE: David, Aiden, Graham, and Roland are the "lunch bunch" and ate
together literally every day of 7th grade. They hang out a lot away from
the field and are excited to all play together.

A Note About Tactics
In 24-25 the head coach was Aaron Greene, whose son Beau Greene is an
absolute freak athlete (so fast) that generally played as our striker. Fall
24 season it was all about getting balls over the top to Beau and letting
him run onto the ball and try to solo the defense. It worked more often
than it should because he is so fast, but against better coached teams or
teams that also have fast players it is obviously less effective. Spring 25
season the team had much more build up play through the middle of the
field, but even from build up the end of almost every action was a pass to
Beau followed by him shooting. Beau is a trapped 8th grader so he will
probably be practicing with the team but can't play with them, which means
Fall 25 is a real opportunity to evolve the games of the rest of the kids.
(Beau also didn't do a whole lot of passing, so your outside mids like
David and Aiden were not always in the habit of running hard to get into
the play.)

Fall 24 we run a 4-1-4-1, with Graham as the middle 1 and Beau as the top
1. Spring 25 it evolved a bit with a central midfielder placed higher up
the field to run with Beau, but I think it would be overstating it to call
the formation a 4-1-3-2. To my eyes it never really looked like the
midfield was sorted, with confusion between the two central mids and the
two outside mids tending to hang back defensively. Teams that were well
coached on their positioning could figure this out and exploit us through
the middle, while stopping a lot of our attacks at midfield because there
was too much space between the mids and the lone forward. So at times it
almost looked like a 4-2-1-2-1 or something, with the 2 outside mids
playing back, Graham running everywhere, 2 central mids that were often too
close together, and then Beau alone at forward.

Anyway, with the kids above that are used to playing that way you will
probably have some work to do to teach them better positioning. Of course
depending on what formation you want to run and which kids you want to play
where. Based on the 6 PYSA kids and 4 NMFC kids, I'm spitballing something
like this:
*4-4-2 Diamond midfield*
9 - Stephen 9 - Aiden
10 - Roland
11 - David 7 - PYSA Grayson (see who adapts better to right)
6 - Graham
LFB ?, CB ?, CB Carter, RFB Theo/?
Carter-Graham-Roland as the spine of the team could be incredible. David
and PYSA Grayson both played a lot of left mid, but also played center and
right, so you would need to see who fits better where. (NMFC Grayson only
plans to be around for fall season AFAIK so I wouldn't start him, even
though I think he is a good player.) Biggest hole is obviously on the
defensive line, you could try playing David at left fullback but its been a
few seasons since he played much defense. IMO Graham's motor would be
wasted playing center back.

*4-3-3? 4-3-2-1?* Attacking midfield triangle.
I think David and Roland could be an effective central midfield pair in
front of Graham. Something like:
11 - PYSA Grayson, 9 - Stephen, 7 - Aiden
8 - David, 10 - Roland
6 - Graham
Back 4.
