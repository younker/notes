OK, here is my take on this election FOR YOUR EYES ONLY. I honestly don't
know a lot of these people so I will comment on those I do know.

PAHA has had a lot of issues since David started playing here, and since I
started coaching and then joined the board. Some of it is just people being
people, plus COVID, but there are some larger issues we need to get a
handle on to create a more sustainable version of PAHA long-term. I don't
know what the answers are, but I do think having 9 volunteers in charge of
everything is a huge burden on those people and helps create the situation
we have now where people don't serve out their terms and/or do a bad job in
their roles on the board. The constant turnover means we have been stuck
figuring it out as we go. I think it is pretty interesting that so many
people are trying to get on the board now, when back during the regular
election period almost no one was interested and a couple people got
re-elected that maybe shouldn't have.

Also I mentioned that as much as I like Fed that I am worried about the
conflict of interest (going both directions) given Fed is the owner of
PAHA's home rink and PAHA is by far his biggest customer. He is also
"campaigning" with that email and kind of named a slate, which some people
thought was weird. Personally I think everyone would be better off if PAHA
and Fed related to each other through a "hockey committee" or something
similar that focused on "on ice" issues and kept each other at arms' length
regarding "off ice" issues. However this is a small town and people seem to
be used to this kind of stuff and therefore are not as worried as me? I am
also the only one whose day job is at a non-profit so that is part of my
perspective as well.

Brittney Case is Zae's mom - I don't really know her but she just had a
baby, and PAHA is a huge time suck so no matter how great she would be I
worry about that.

Katie Connolly has younger kids in PAHA, her dad coaches at Mini level and
she organizes all his paperwork so she has some skills in that area and
some understanding of what is going on.

Diana Guiney - I don't know her but as you can see she has been involved in
one way or another for a long time. Her husband is the current high school
coach.

Rob Higgins - the former high school coach, he is Fed's right hand man at
FHA. Great guy and a great coach.

Charlie Licata - he has been around PAHA for a long time, including
formerly as President. Its been told to me that a lot of the issues between
PAHA and Fed arose during Charlie's time at the helm of PAHA, so it was
funny to see Fed vouch for Charlie in his email. (I don't have first hand
knowledge of any of that.) I do know that people either love or hate
Charlie, and he has a strong personality, so while I think he is a good guy
he may not be who we need on the board right now.

Henry Pfeifer - great guy, I play with him in B League where he is one of
the guys running the show. He has younger kids and I think could be great
as someone who could get involved and stay involved for a long time.

Special Election Announcements

Dear Valued Members,

As many of you now know, we will be holding a special election to fill the
vacant board positions.  We do not ask our volunteers to campaign for the
positions however due to recent events and a multitude of questions from
candidates and members the board would like to send out some additional
information to all of you before sending the ballot.

We have three open positions on the board due to several individuals’ who
became unable to complete their term.  All board positions require a large
time commitment, so we appreciate everyone who stepped up to volunteer!
  These positions would be filled to complete the current term thus two of
the positions filled would be up again for reelection during our normal
election period this summer, along with 2 other current standing board
members.  Additionally,  our bylaws leave the board open to one seat for a
non-PAHA parent.  There are several candidates who do not currently have a
child registered to participate in the organization.  Those individuals are
noted below.  Should several non-parent candidates be voted we will take
the one with the largest number of votes.

We would like everyone to take a minute and review ALL OUR CANDIDATES and
consider them equally for these positions.   We can assure that all these
individuals share the same love, passion, and dedication toward the
advancement of hockey in our association.  We would like to give everyone
some time to review these amazing candidates, enjoy the holiday weekend,
and then expect the ballot via email on Monday.

*Brittney Case *- Having two of my children actively participating in the
program I am looking for a way to get more involved with the association. I
am very passionate about the success of all levels of the program and would
like the opportunity to play a crucial part in ensuring we offer great
hockey to everyone.  I have seen great things happening in the association
and would like to extend my time and energy to continue fostering positive
growth in our organization.  I love to be an active part of my kids'
extracurricular activities and hope you will consider me to fill a position
on the board.

*Katie Connolly - *Growing up in Harbor Springs, Michigan, and now residing
in downtown Petoskey, hockey has been an integral part of my life with a
family legacy deeply rooted in the sport.  Given my lifelong connection to
hockey, from the days of playing outside at the Winter Sports Park to the
current rink, and with both of my sons actively participating in the sport,
I am genuinely passionate about contributing to the growth and maintenance
of our local hockey program.  I would be honored to serve on the board and
kindly request that you consider me during the selection process. I
believe my personal history with the game, combined with my commitment to
fostering a small-town family atmosphere within the program, aligns well
with the values and goals of the board.

*Mike English* - Hi my name is Mike and I've been playing hockey since I
was a Mite and have coached for the last 4 years. I'm just looking to share
the sport I love and help where I can. Thank you for your consideration.

*Fedor Fedorov* - I feel my experience in the sport of hockey would be
beneficial to the parents and players of PAHA. I played professional hockey
for 17 years before retiring to northern Michigan. I have trained 1000’s of
players throughout my career as owner of the Fedorov Hockey Academy. The
past 6 years I have invested my time, energy, and financial support into
the Petoskey Ice Arena to make it a great place for players to train and
play. My mission is to grow youth hockey and help players improve their
skill. My two sons will be growing up and playing here, so I am passionate
about making the program the best place for all hockey players to learn the
sport.

*Diana Guiney* - I currently serve as a MAHA District 7 Director starting
in 2018-present, I served as a member of the MAHA District 7 Council from
2014-2017 and I was previously a serving board member for Petoskey Area
Hockey Association from 2011-2017 in the following positions: President,
Registrar and Fundraising Coordinator.  I have found the urge to get back
involved and serve our younger generation of hockey players.  I would
appreciate your consideration and am hopeful for the opportunity to
contribute to PAHA again.  (Does not currently have child in program)

*Katlyn Hansen* - Born and raised in Petoskey, both my husband Billy Hansen
and I participated in Petoskey sports and now have one of two children
participating. Our families are strong advocates and supporters for
Petoskey athletics, including hockey as our daughter is now involved. I am
a business administrator for a local nonprofit organization. My
responsibilities include financials, fundraisers, and general business
operations and policies. I have served previously as President and am a
current board member of Kiwanis of LTB.  (Does not currently have child in
program)

*Rob Higgins *- I’m Rob Higgins, and I believe I bring a diverse
professional background with a passion for hockey and youth development. As
a Trainer at the Fedorov Hockey Academy, I actively engage with young
athletes, focusing on refining their hockey skills like skating, passing,
and shooting. My role as the former Head and Assistant Coach for the
Petoskey Varsity Hockey team was centered around enhancing on-ice
strategies and overall student-athlete growth. Prior to my coaching
experience, I played a variety of levels in hockey ranging from house as a
youth to NCAA as a collegiate athlete. (Does not currently have child in
program)

*Charles Licata *- Charlie has been an active volunteer for PAHA the last
nine years in multiple leadership positions.  He has a passion for teaching
hockey and leading organizations.  Over the past nine years he has gained
valuable experience in how MAHA and USA hockey operates.  Charlie has a
vast network of hockey contacts within these organizations that can help
build PAHA into a premier hockey organization.  Developing the next
generation of leaders is the goal in order to build a long-term sustainable
Organization.

*Henry Pfeifer *- My name is Henry Pfeifer. My family and I live in
Petoskey and are involved with hockey and skating on many levels. My
daughter Charlotte is a Squirt this year, Florence is a figure skater, and
Henry, who’s in his second year of skating, will be a Mini Mite next year.
I’ve helped run, and played in, the adult hockey league for 10 years and
feel I would be a great fit for helping PAHA with the next generation.
Thank you.

*Joe Placido* - My name is Joe, I’m 45 years old and my son, Henry, is on
the 8U/2015 team. I am an active dad and have experience as a youth coach.
I’m a Navy veteran and I have experience in developmental leadership which
I like to use to strengthen team camaraderie.  I also have a lot of
experience in emergency response so I will be good at keeping an eye on
safety issues throughout PAHA.  (I was even a Paramedic for the Motor City
Chiefs hockey team).  I think I could be useful to the PAHA community.
Thank you for considering me.

*Dave Thom* - David Thom Jr. is a community leader and hockey enthusiast
with a background in forensic psychology and emergency management. He is
committed to using his experience to help the Petoskey Area Hockey
Association (PAHA) grow and become an elite hockey program. He believes
that hockey is more than just a sport; it is a way to teach young people
important life skills. He is also committed to creating a safe and
inclusive environment for all players and families.

As a final note, we want to express our appreciation for all of you!  Our
association wouldn’t be what it is without each of you and your wonderful
children.  We hope you have safe and wonderful holiday weekend, and we will
see you at the rink next week!


With Gratitude,
